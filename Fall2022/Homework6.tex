\section{Bremsstrahlung}
In the lecture, the IR divergence of the electron vertex is given by
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned} \delta F_{1}^{\textrm{ren}}(q^{2}) &=\delta F_{1}(q^{2})-\delta F_{1}(0)=\frac{\alpha}{2 \pi} f_{\mathrm{IR}}(q^{2}) \times \log \left(\frac{-q^{2}}{\mu^{2}}\right)+\text { regular terms } \\ f_{\mathrm{IR}}(q^{2}) &=-1+\int_{0}^{1} d \xi\left(\frac{m^{2}-q^{2} / 2}{m^{2}-q^{2} \xi(1-\xi)}\right) ~.\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
This cancels with the infrared singularity in the \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha)\) cross-section for a single photon emission,
$$
d \sigma\left[e^{-}(p) \rightarrow e^{-}\left(p^{\prime}\right)+\gamma\right]=d \sigma_{0}\left[e^{-}(p) \rightarrow e^{-}\left(p^{\prime}\right)\right] \times \frac{\alpha}{\pi} I(\beta, \beta^{\prime}) \times \frac{d E_{\gamma}}{E_{\gamma}}
$$
where
$$
I(\beta, \beta^{\prime})=\int \frac{d \Omega_{\boldsymbol{k}}}{4 \pi}|\boldsymbol{k}|^{2}\left[\frac{2 p \cdot p^{\prime}}{(k \cdot p)\left(k \cdot p^{\prime}\right)}-\frac{m^{2}}{(k \cdot p)^{2}}-\frac{m^{2}}{\left(k \cdot p^{\prime}\right)^{2}}\right]~.
$$
Show that \(2 f_{\mathrm{IR}}(q^{2})=I(\beta, \beta^{\prime})\).

\section{Optical theorem in massless QED}

\begin{equation}\nonumber
\sigma=\frac{1}{s} \operatorname{Im}(\mathcal{M})
\end{equation}
where \(i \mathcal{M}\) the unpolarized (average) Feynman amplitude of the process \(A(k_{1}) B(k_{2}) \rightarrow A(k_{1}) B(k_{2})\).

In the lecture, we have seen that the vacuum polarization takes the form
\begin{equation}\nonumber
\Pi^{\mu \nu}(q)=\left(q^{2} g^{\mu \nu}-q^{\mu} q^{\nu}\right) \Pi(q^{2})
\end{equation}
Also, the dimensional regularization $d=4-\epsilon$ provides
\begin{equation}\nonumber
\Pi(q^{2})=-\frac{2 \alpha}{\pi} \int_{0}^{1} d x x(1-x)\left(\frac{2}{\epsilon}-\gamma+\log (4 \pi)-\log \frac{-x(1-x) q^{2}-i \tilde{\epsilon}}{\mu^{2}}\right)
\end{equation}
where we replace the electric charge $e$ by $e\mu^\epsilon$.

Show that in the case of \(e^{-} e^{+} \rightarrow \mu^{-}\mu^{+}\) scattering in CM-frame (\(m_{e}=m_{\mu}=0\)) the unpolarized
cross-section (order \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha^{2}))\) is given by
$$
\sigma=\frac{4 \pi \alpha^{2}}{3 s}
$$
using the optical theorem and the expression for \(\operatorname{Im}(\Pi(q^{2}))\).
